# Old School 93/94 Card Power Spectrum

Synthesized from Eternal Central, oldschool-mtg.blogspot.com, This Old Deck, The Wizard's Tower, and tournament results across a decade of 93/94 play.

Cards are organized by function and power tier. Cards in the same tier are roughly interchangeable in terms of how much they warp games.

---

## Tier 0 — The Restricted List (broken, one-of only)

Restricted precisely because they're in a different power class from everything else. The format would be solved around them if unrestricted.

### Mana Acceleration / Free Mana
- Black Lotus
- Mox Pearl, Mox Sapphire, Mox Jet, Mox Ruby, Mox Emerald
- Sol Ring
- Channel

### Card Advantage Engines
- **Library of Alexandria** — considered the most powerful card in the format by experienced players, more impactful than most of the Power Nine in practice because it generates repeated advantage rather than one burst
- Ancestral Recall
- Braingeyser
- Timetwister
- Wheel of Fortune

### Broken Effects
- Time Walk (extra turn for 2 mana)
- Demonic Tutor (unrestricted tutoring for 2)
- Mind Twist (hand destruction at variable cost)
- Mana Drain (counter + free mana)
- Balance (asymmetric board reset)
- Regrowth (recursion for 2)
- Time Vault (the combo engine)

## Tier 1 — The Pillars (4-of staples that define decks)

Legal at 4 copies but appear in virtually every top-tier deck that can cast them.

### Lands
- **Strip Mine** — free mana denial, goes in every deck
- **Mishra's Factory** — threat that dodges creature removal
- **Maze of Ith** — the best blocker ever printed
- Dual lands (Revised): Underground Sea, Volcanic Island, Tropical Island, etc. — not broken individually but their scarcity makes the format's mana architecture fragile and interesting

### Mana Fixing
- City of Brass
- Fellwar Stone

### Removal
- **Swords to Plowshares** — most efficient creature removal in the format, exiles for W
- **Lightning Bolt** — 3 damage for R, also doubles as reach/direct damage
- **Disenchant** — critical given the artifact-heavy metagame
- Terror (black removal, more restrictive than Swords)

### Counterspells
- Counterspell (the backbone of blue control; Mana Drain is restricted, this is the next best thing)
- Power Sink

### Top Creatures
- **Serendib Efreet** — 2U for a 3/4 flyer; in nearly every blue deck. 4 toughness laughs at Lightning Bolt. The defining blue threat.
- **Juzam Djinn** — 4-mana 5/5 with negligible downside; way above curve for the environment. Money cost keeps it out of many builds more than anything else.
- **Serra Angel** — 4/4 vigilant flyer; the classic control finisher. Double-white limits multicolor builds.

## Tier 2 — Format Staples (widely played, not universal)

Define archetypes. Consistently appear in top-8 results.

### Creatures
- **Kird Ape** — R for a 2/3; once banned; cornerstone of Zoo and RG Beats
- **Hypnotic Specter** — 2-power flyer that attacks the hand; cornerstone of black aggro; must-answer threat
- **Erhnam Djinn** — the green midrange threat; often paired with Armageddon (the "ErhnamGeddon" archetype)
- **Triskelion** — 6 mana 4/4; slow, but distributing 3 damage anywhere gives it incredible versatility
- **Savannah Lions** — 2/1 for W; White Weenie cornerstone
- **Hypnotic Specter** — a 2-power flyer that can rip apart a hand; cornerstone of all black-based decks

### Draw / Card Advantage (non-restricted)
- **Sylvan Library** — green's answer to blue draw spells; repeatedly filters draws
- **Jayemdae Tome** — slow but reliable card advantage
- **Hymn to Tourach** — 2-mana hand disruption (EC rules; not legal in Swedish)
- Psionic Blast — 4 damage for 2 mana at the cost of 2 life; blue's pseudo-burn and reach

### Disruption
- Dark Ritual — mana acceleration into explosive turns
- Sinkhole — land destruction at 2 mana
- Stone Rain — 3-mana land destruction
- Armageddon — mass land destruction; enables ErhnamGeddon and Deadguy archetypes
- **Blood Moon** — sideboard bomb often maindecked; punishes the greedy multicolor mana bases that define the format

### Artifacts
- Icy Manipulator — repeatable tap effect; goes wide
- Nevinyrral's Disk — board wipe on a stick; slow but universal
- Black Vise — burn for full hands; punishes slow draws and control opponents
- The Rack — paired with discard in mono-black prison strategies

## Tier 3 — Archetype Enablers (playable, but conditional)

Strong within a specific archetype; not cross-format.

### Spells
- Animate Dead / Reincarnation — reanimation package (thin in 93/94 but the core of the archetype)
- Copy Artifact — combo and value loops
- Winter Orb — prison strategy with Icy Manipulator
- Chaos Orb — restricted but unique; destroys any permanent on a skilled flip; one of the few cards you can play in Old School that you literally can't play in Vintage

### Lands
- Mishra's Workshop — powers artifact-heavy decks
- Bazaar of Baghdad — fuels reanimator and Lich-style strategies

### Creatures
- **Su-Chi** — 4/4 artifact creature that generates 4 mana on death; cornerstone of artifact-heavy decks; the "budget Juzam" that isn't really budget
- Sedge Troll — regenerating 2/2 for 3 in RB
- Juggernaut — 5/3 trample for 4; solid budget threat
- White Knight / Black Knight — protection from the opposing primary removal color; solid in aggro mirrors

## Tier 4 — Playable Filler and Sideboard

Good in specific matchups or as budget inclusions.

### Color Hate (Sideboard Staples)
- Blue Elemental Blast — counters or destroys anything red; premier blue sideboard card
- Red Elemental Blast — counters or destroys anything blue; premier red sideboard card

### Artifact Answers
- Shatter / Detonate
- Shatterstorm — one-sided sweeper when you build around it
- Crumble
- Energy Flux — shuts down artifact-heavy decks at the engine level
- Dust to Dust — exile two artifacts for 1WW; better than Shatter in white builds

### Enchantment Answers
- Tranquility — wipes enchantments; relevant against Stasis and prison builds

### Creatures
- Birds of Paradise / Llanowar Elves — outclassed by Sol Ring and Moxen but foundational in green builds without Power
- Whirling Dervish — grows in black matchups; solid sideboard threat
- Scryb Sprites / Argothian Pixies — flying and protection utility in green
- Serendib Djinn — poor man's Serendib Efreet; the downside (sacrifice land or sacrifice self) is real

---

## Effectively Unplayable in Competitive Builds

- Most of Fallen Empires — the set was so underpowered it barely shows up
- Most creatures above 4 mana without a busted effect
- Multi-step enchantments with no immediate impact
- Anything requiring a complex setup that faster decks can goldfish through

## Calibration Notes

**The format's power context matters.** These aren't just abstract ratings — the meta is defined by a small set of dominant archetypes:

| Archetype | Core Cards |
|---|---|
| The Deck | Counterspell, Mana Drain, Jayemdae Tome, Serra Angel, Disenchant, Swords |
| ErhnamGeddon | Erhnam Djinn, Armageddon, Savannah Lions |
| Troll Disco | Sedge Troll, Nevinyrral's Disk, Dark Ritual |
| UR Burn / Electric Eel Aggro | Lightning Bolt, Chain Lightning, Serendib Efreet, Psionic Blast |
| Monoblack | Juzam Djinn, Hypnotic Specter, Dark Ritual, Sinkhole, Mind Twist |
| Zoo / RG Aggro | Kird Ape, Erhnam Djinn, Lightning Bolt, Chain Lightning |
| Power Monolith | Basalt Monolith + Power Artifact combo |
| Atog Burn | Atog, Black Vise, artifact fodder, burn |

In unified team play (where each card can only appear once across three decks), card evaluation shifts noticeably: without Mishra's Factories, smaller creatures improve; without Disenchants, combos get better; without counterspells, the field opens up significantly.
